# Judd Antin - 播客摘要

**嘉宾简介**: Judd Antin - 前Facebook用户研究负责人、前Airbnb设计工作室负责人,咨询顾问,帮助公司处理组织挑战、产品策略、设计研究、招聘、入职和危机管理

---

## 核心议题

1. **用户研究领域的"清算时刻"** - 为什么UX研究在过去15年走向了错误方向,需要如何evolve?
2. **Middle Range Research陷阱** - 为什么最常做的用户研究类型impact最低却最常被request?
3. **"User-Centered Performance"现象** - 当团队假装用户驱动但实际上只是为了perform customer obsession时会发生什么?
4. **研究人员作为合作伙伴而非服务** - 如何重构research与product的关系以maximize impact?

---

## 核心洞察

### 新颖洞察

- **"The User Research Reckoning is Here"** - Layoffs hitting UX research disproportionately不是coincidence而是symptom of broken system。Research被set up as service function,not integrated,导致low impact然后sidelined/laid off。问题不是research没价值而是current system broken

- **"Macro-Middle-Micro Framework"** - Research分为3层:
  - **Macro**: Strategic,business-focused,forward-looking(competitive landscape,TAM studies,concept cars like "Insights 2020")
  - **Micro**: Laser-focused usability,A/B test interpretation,quick wins(48h turnaround,"multimillion dollar button"案例)
  - **Middle**: The blobular zone where most questions live("How do hosts feel about payment options?")。Interesting but not impactful enough,not aligned to funnel/OKRs

- **"We don't validate, we falsify"** - Good research mindset是looking to be wrong而非validating assumptions。Most PMs/designers don't want to be wrong,所以research变成check-the-box performance而非learning tool

- **"N=1 can blow minds"** - Statistical significance not always needed。One "super hider"在Facebook user study彻底改变团队对"hiding"signal的理解。Sometimes one extreme example足够burst bubble and enable better solution

### 具体比喻

- **"Swiss Army Knife researcher"** - Best researchers有5 tools: formative/generative research, evaluative/usability, survey design, applied statistics, SQL/prompt engineering。只懂qualitative不够,需要multi-method

- **"User-centered performance as symbolic vs. learning"** - 像corporate theater:做research是为了signal "we're customer obsessed"而非actually make different decisions。"Can you just run a quick study to validate our assumptions?" = too late to matter,just check box

- **"Super hiders"** - Facebook发现的extreme users who hide everything on newsfeed。Initial assumption: hiding signal = bad content。Reality: one user's model was "inbox zero" for social feed。这n=1完全changed team's mental model

- **"If a tree fell in the forest and no one was there to hear it"** - Research insights不communicate effectively = 没impact。Need to speak right language to right audience (CEO vs CFO vs工程师)

### 保留的英文术语

- **Formative vs. Evaluative research** - Generative/innovative vs. usability/testing
- **Post-hoc bias** - "We knew that already" / "That was obvious" → research undervalued
- **Wisdom of the crowd** - Diverse perspectives beat individual gut, but requires different information sources
- **Narrative fallacy** - People love simple stories about the past, twist evidence to fit narrative
- **Window of tolerance** (虽然来自Jonny Miller但relevant)

## 心法与原则

1. **"Don't validate, falsify"** - Research mindset应该是"I want to be wrong, tell me how and why"而非"tell me I'm right"。这是countering confirmation bias和ego的关键

2. **"Research should be in the room from beginning to end"** - Metric for success: "they won't have that meeting without the researcher." When research is reactive service,already too late to shape questions

3. **"Fewer things, better"** - Researcher covering 7 PMs and 20 designers = no one gets good work。Better to pair researcher with few partners consistently,create pain for others who want access

4. **"Business language is research language"** - Researchers必须read quarterly reports,understand OKRs,know the metrics and funnel。Speak the language or stay irrelevant

5. **"The gut is where bias lives"** - Intuition valuable but also where all cognitive biases dwell。Need System 2 thinking + research to check gut,not just trust it blindly

## 实战手册

### 识别并避免"Middle Range Research"陷阱

**Middle Range的Red Flags:**
- Questions like "How do users feel about X feature?" without linking to business problem
- Interesting to hear but hard to operationalize
- Not framed in funnel/strategy/OKR language
- Yields results where people say "we knew that already"

**如何转化为High-Impact Research:**

**Option A: Push to Micro (快速战术)**
- Narrow to specific usability question
- Example: "Why aren't people clicking this CTA?" → 48h research发现button text scary,7个字符change增加1% conversion(millions of dollars)
- Timeline: 1-7 days depending on depth
- When: Need quick win,clear hypothesized problem

**Option B: Elevate to Macro (战略规划)**
- Tie to annual planning,competitive landscape,strategy
- Example: "Insights 2020" concept car project at Airbnb - spent months synthesizing all year's insights to imagine 5-year future
- When: Quarterly/annual planning cycles,portfolio strategy

**Option C: Make Middle Range Pointed (对齐业务)**
- Same question但frame in business terms
- "How do hosts feel about payment options?" → "Where in the funnel is payment flexibility impacting host retention?"
- Researcher proactively connects to metrics/OKRs
- When: Question valuable but needs business context

### 作为PM如何最大化Research Impact

**Integrate Researchers End-to-End:**
- Involve from ideation,not just before ship
- Make them part of core team,shared OKRs
- Example: Lenny went to Paris with researcher Louise for in-field studies,behind-mirror observations

**Ask the Right Questions:**
- 不要:"Can you validate our assumptions?" (too late)
- 要:"Here's the problem I'm trying to solve,what's the best way to understand it?"
- 不要:"Can we do a quick study?" (sounds like check box)
- 要:"We have this hypothesis,how can we falsify it?"

**Prioritize Together:**
- Researcher bandwidth is scarce (3 things max: 2 big + 1 small)
- Work with researcher to prioritize what matters most
- Help them say no to low-impact requests

**Participate in Research:**
- Go to field visits,travel with team
- Be behind the mirror during sessions
- You'll learn things no report can capture

### 招聘优秀Researcher的标准

**5-Tool Swiss Army Knife:**
1. **Formative/Generative Research** - Ethnographic,open-ended,innovation-focused
2. **Evaluative/Usability** - Micro-level,testing specific implementations
3. **Survey Design** - Rigorous methodology,not just "let me ask some questions"
4. **Applied Statistics** - Can interact with A/B test results,understand significance
5. **Technical Skills** - SQL for querying own data OR prompt engineering for AI tools

**Interview Techniques:**
- Give juicy,open-ended research scenario
- Look for: "Here are 2-3 ways we could handle this,depending on timeframe (day/week/month)"
- Green flag: Multi-method answer,acknowledges tradeoffs
- Red flag: "Here's THE way to do it" (one-tool approach)

**Key Traits:**
- Communication skills (如果tree fell in forest...)
- Business acumen (can speak language of product/metrics)
- Influence (can partner without authority)
- T-shaped depth in one tool but breadth across all

### 避免"User-Centered Performance"

**Warning Signs:**
- "Quick user study to validate" at end of process
- Executive listening sessions as performance
- "We already knew that" responses to research findings
- Research used to justify decisions already made

**What to Do Instead:**
- Bring research in early when questions still being shaped
- Focus on learning,not validating
- Ask "what could prove us wrong?" not "what will prove us right?"
- Reward teams for changing direction based on insights

### 搭建高效Research Organization

**Structure:**
- **不要**: Separate research silos (UX research,market research,consumer insights,customer feedback)
- **要**: Integrated insights function combining all disciplines under one leader
- Example at Airbnb: Brought in NPS program,customer service feedback,data science,UX research into one "insights machine"

**Sizing:**
- No clean "ratio" answer
- Guiding principle: Relationships over coverage
- Pair researcher with consistent partners who will involve them
- Create pain of loss: "I wish I had research support" → advocates for headcount
- Always room for researcher: even early startups can benefit (1/20 companies surveyed had researcher in first 10 hires)

**Career Paths:**
- Researchers need development paths like everyone else
- Opportunity to build skills in business,communication,leadership
- Not just "individual contributor forever" tracks
- Can lead to product,design,or general management roles

### Research与Data Science的完美合作

**The virtuous cycle:**
- A/B test shows stat sig change → Data says "what happened?"
- Research answers "why did it happen?" → Qualitative insights
- Combined insights → Better hypothesis → Next test
- Repeat

**Example Partnership:**
- Data: "Conversion dropped 5% with new design"
- Research: "Users find button language threatening,scared to click"
- Together: "Test reassuring language vs. current" → faster learning than endless A/B testing alone

**How to Enable:**
- Shared OKRs (researcher + PM + data scientist on same metrics)
- Regular syncs to review experiments and plan next steps
- Research proactively reaches out when sees test opportunity
- Data involves research early when designing experiments

### 应对常见Research Trope

**Trope 1: "Research is too slow"**
- Reality: Good research can go fast (48h for micro)
- Speed depends on question depth (day/week/month options)
- Counter: Is it slower to get it wrong then fix? Quick research prevents bigger mistakes

**Trope 2: "I can do my own research"**
- Reality: Anyone can talk to users ≠ good research
- Problem: "Garbage in,garbage out" - one user idiosyncratic
- Value: Researcher knows how to get to heart quickly,situate findings in broader context

**Trope 3: "A/B test everything"**
- Reality: A/B tests tell you what happened,not why
- Problem: Teams speculate endlessly about results
- Value: Research provides "why" that prevents repeated mistakes

**Trope 4: "Henry Ford said faster horses"**
- Reality: He never said that (apocryphal)
- Problem: Assumes researchers ask "what do you want?"
- Reality: Good researchers never ask that

**Trope 5: "We already knew that" (Post-hoc bias)**
- Reality: Hindsight bias,we construct narratives after fact
- Problem: Devalues research insights
- Solution: Document predictions beforehand,compare to findings

### NPS的替代方案

**Why NPS Fails:**
- 11-point scale (precision drops after 5-7 items)
- Usually unlabeled (not gold standard)
- "Likelihood to recommend" = wrong question for many products (who recommends OS?)
- Idiosyncratic and inconsistent (can't compare across companies)
- Survey science consensus: NPS makes all mistakes

**Better Alternative: CSAT**
- Question: "Overall,how satisfied are you with your experience with [product]?"
- Better statistical properties
- More correlated with business outcomes
- More precise (fewer items)
- Works on mobile (all options visible)

**At Airbnb's Validation:**
- Redid work to test NPS vs CSAT for Airbnb specifically
- Found: CSAT better aligned with business metrics
- Recommendation: Don't ask NPS,ask satisfaction

## 反常识视角

1. **"Too much research on 'interesting but not impactful' questions"** - Middle range research feels interesting (user psychology!)但business impact低。Layoffs部分because companies realize they're paying for research that doesn't drive decisions

2. **"Best researchers should be business-focused"** - Anti-capitalist critique of "profit focus" misses point: If research not helping business succeed,what are we doing? Need to find overlap between user needs and business goals

3. **"You don't need statistical significance for impact"** - One user (super hider) changed entire team's mental model at Facebook。Qualitative insights can be more powerful than quantitative if they shift paradigm

4. **"Executives love middle range research because it enables their gut decisions"** - They can cite "research" while still deciding based on opinion。Research becomes rubber stamp而非counterweight

5. **"Dogfooding your product is necessary but insufficient"** - PMs should use their product BUT they're not representative users。Prioritizing issues found via dogfooding requires external validation - your constraints/priorities differ wildly from average user

## 总结

Judd Antin的"User Research Reckoning"是对UX研究领域的犀利wake-up call。His核心argument: Research layoffs不是bad economy而是symptom of broken system where research被set up to fail。

**The Problem:**
- Research被当作reactive service function,not integrated partner
- Too much "middle range research" - interesting but not aligned to business
- "User-centered performance" - research used to signal customer obsession,not drive learning
- Researchers lack business acumen,can't speak language of metrics/OKRs

**The Solution:**
- Evolve to "new version of research" - more impactful,business-aware,integrated
- Develop diverse skills (5-tool Swiss army knife approach)
- Focus on macro (strategic) and micro (quick wins),minimize middle
- Integrate researchers into product process from beginning to end
- Create unified insights function combining UX research,data science,customer feedback

**For PMs:**
- Involve researchers early,not just before ship
- Ask "how can we falsify this?" not "can you validate this?"
- Participate in research,go to field visits
- Help prioritize research workload (researchers overcommitted)
- Learn to partner with researchers as equals,not service providers

**For Researchers:**
- Learn business language,read quarterly reports,understand OKRs
- Develop communication skills - "if tree fell in forest..."
- Push back on low-impact middle range requests
- Focus on fewer things,do them better
- Build relationships so teams can't meet without you

The reckoning isn't death butevolution。Research more crucial than ever但needs to adapt to new reality。Great researchers make teams go faster,not slower,and answer questions impossible to answer otherwise。Future is bright for those who evolve。
